# Project Play 2024 National Youth Sports Parent Survey — Complete Data Index

**Source document:** `National-Youth-Sports-Parent-Survey-FINAL.pdf` (this folder)
**Full title:** *2024 Parenting Survey — Final Report*
**Commissioned by:** The Aspen Institute, Sports & Society Program / **Project Play** (63x30 national table)
**Analyses by:** Dr. Travis E. Dorsch (Families in Sport Lab, Utah State University) & Dr. Jordan A. Blazo (Louisiana Tech University)
**Field dates:** November 5 – December 19, 2024 · Hosted by Qualtrics

## CHAPTER 2 — THE NATIONAL SAMPLE

### 2.1 Parent demographics (p.7–8)
- **Age:** M = 40.84 (range 18–86).
- **Sex:** 58.6% female, 41.1% male, 0.3% non-binary.
- **Race/ethnicity:** White 62.4%, Black 20.9%, Hispanic 8.7%, Asian 4.0%, multiracial 1.6%, AI/AN 1.3%, NH/PI 0.4%.
- **Employment:** full-time 61.9%, part-time 10.4%, homemaker 10.3%, self-employed 5.6%, retired 3.8%, unable to work 3.5%, out of work 2.3%, student 1.8%.

### 2.2 Household demographics (p.9–11)
- **States:** all 50 + DC. Most represented: NY (338, 18.3%), CO (225, 12.2%), TX (117), FL (105), CA (92), OH (88). Top 6 = 53.2% of sample (CO/NYC oversampled by design).
- **Region:** Northeast 26.7%, Southeast 26.1%, West 20.8%, Midwest 17.3%, Southwest 9.1%.
- **Community:** Suburban 43.1%, Urban 42.0%, Rural 14.9%.
- **Annual household income:** Mean **$88,258**, **median $63,000** (≈ national median), SD $113,642, range $0–$1.99M.
- **Children ≤18 in household:** M = 1.79 (range 1–10). **Children playing sports in last year:** M = 1.58.

### 2.3 Athlete demographics (p.12–13)
- **Child age:** M = 11.59 (range 6–18). Largest single age = 6 yrs (13.9%).
- **Child sex:** 60.8% male, 39.1% female, 0.1% non-binary.
- **Child race/ethnicity:** White 58.0%, Black 21.2%, Hispanic 9.9%, multiracial 5.3%, Asian 3.4%.
- ★ **Documented disability: 23.1%** (≈ 1 in 4). Among disabilities: ADHD 20.3% (most common named), learning 10.3%, vision 9.5%, physical 8.7%, ASD 7.5%, intellectual 5.7%, hearing 3.5%, chronic disease 1.5%, "other" 47.8%.

## CHAPTER 3 — DESCRIPTIVE STATISTICS (universal, full sample)

### 3.1 Sport participation (p.15–22)
- **Most-played sports (last year):** Basketball 41.9%, Soccer 24.1%, Baseball 23.1%, Tackle football 16.3%, Dance 10.9%, Flag football 10.6%. (Football flag+tackle combined = **26.9%**.)
- **Primary sport:** Basketball 22.8%, Soccer 14.6%, Baseball 10.0%, Tackle football 7.8%; flag/tackle = 10.9% combined.
- **Settings (primary sport):** Community-based 42.6%, Free play 41.3%, Interscholastic 40.4%, Intramural 29.9%, Travel/elite/club 17.3%, Independent training 14.1%.
- **Months/yr in scheduled play:** M = 5.55. 47.8% play 1–4 months; **20.5% play 9–12 months** (near year-round); 9.6% play all 12 months.
- **Days/week in-season (by type):** Coach-led practice 2.66, Individual practice 2.10, Free play 1.96, Games 1.80 (total engagement 8.52).
- ★ **Well-being WHEN engaged (increases/increases greatly):** Physical fitness **84.4%**, Social well-being 79.8%, Mental health **75.1%**, Emotional control 73.5%.
- ★ **Well-being WHEN NOT engaged (decreases/decreases greatly):** Physical fitness 32.7%, Social 24.4%, Emotional 23.8%, Mental health 27.1% (≈ 1 in 4 across measures).
- ★ **Pressure to SPECIALIZE (some + a lot):** from own child 53.7% (24.6% "a lot"), school coaches ~49%, society 49.8%, other parents ~48%, club coaches ~47%. **Majority feel ≥ some pressure.**
- ★ **Goals that "justify" the specialization pressure:** play in **high school 53.1%**, **college 34.7%**, **professionally 24.8%**, Olympian/national team 18.6%; "pressure is NOT appropriate" only **16.4%**.
- **Multi-sport policy — school programs:** encouraged 58.4%, discouraged 10.6%, prohibited 5.1%.
- **Multi-sport policy — club/non-school:** encouraged 52.4%, discouraged 11.9%, prohibited 5.7%.
- ★ **Level parents BELIEVE child can reach:** high school **57.5%**, recreational 42.5%, elite club/travel 22.7%, lower-level college 22.7%, higher-level college 22.2%, **professional 11.4%**, national/Olympic/Paralympic 10.9%.

### 3.2 Parent involvement (p.23–25)
- **Volunteer with team/club:** **61.6%** (6 in 10). Roles: "team parent" 43.6%, concessions 16.4%, coach 14.5%, referee/official 7.1%, administrator 6.4%, trainer/medical 4.2%.
- **Volunteer hours/week:** total M = **4.21** (team parent 2.05, coach 0.78; range up to 43 hrs).
- ★ **Time per sport-day (minutes), both parents:** **Attending practices/games 65.2**, Equipment/laundry 30.3, Driving 27.6, Talking about sport 26.6, Parent-to-parent comms 20.1, Meal prep 18.2, Coach comms 16.9 → **TOTAL 202.7 min ≈ 3.4 hrs/day**.
- ★ **Meals out/week due to sports:** M = **2.52**; 56.3% eat out 2–4×; **13.2% eat out 5–7×**; only 11.9% eat out zero.
- ★ **Sports → less healthy eating:** **35.9% yes**, 56.5% no, 7.6% don't know.
- ★ **Spend — PRIMARY sport (avg/yr):** Travel/lodging **$278**, Registration $197, Lessons $183, Equipment $165, Camps $148, Other $43 → **TOTAL $1,015.64** (range $0–**$24,600**). *Travel is the single biggest line item.*
- **Spend — OTHER sports (avg/yr):** TOTAL **$475.02** (range $0–$25,500). *(Primary + other ≈ $1,491/child.)*

### 3.3 Sport settings (p.26–30)
- **Satisfaction (% satisfied/very):** self as parent 79.2%, child's coach 77.6%, organization 77.4%, local opportunities 76.9%, local facilities 70.9%, **U.S. sport culture 68.5%** (parents rate entities worse the more distal from the family).
- **Most-valued coaching PHILOSOPHIES (rank, lower = more important):** health/fitness (3.33) and fair playing time (3.64) on top; **"winning" (7.00) and "safe environment to prevent injuries" (7.39) ranked LAST.**
- **Most-wanted coach TRAININGS:** injury prevention (2.48) and CPR/first-aid (3.15) on top; trauma-informed practice (10.92) and life-skills (9.62) last.
- ★ **Background check SHOULD be mandatory: 87.9% yes.**
- ★ **Coach actually passed a background check: 66% yes / 10% no / 24% "I don't know."** (1 in 3 parents can't confirm.)
- ★ **Child subjected to inappropriate pressure/exploitation by coaches or adults (past year): 20.5% yes** (1 in 5); 4.7% "don't know."
- **Satisfaction with coach support of child's well-being:** ~77–83% satisfied across mental health, fitness, emotional control, social.
- **Fair playing-time belief:** "all should get some time" 45.1%; "equal time" 19.9%; "only the best" 10.8%. (Coaches' actual policy: "all get some" 39.9%.)
- ★ **Days lost to climate (past year):** very hot temps 2.40, changing winters 2.08, flooding 1.30, wildfire/smoke 1.28 → **TOTAL ≈ 6.93 days** (about a week).
- ★ **Would move child's primary-sport SEASON if weather keeps disrupting: 47.9% yes**, 35.3% no, 16.7% don't know.

## CHAPTER 4 — GROUP DIFFERENCES (selected newsworthy splits)

### 4.1 By community type (Urban / Suburban / Rural) — p.32–44
- Urban parents feel the most specialization pressure and have the highest belief their child can reach D1/Olympic/pro.
- ★ **More than 1 in 4 URBAN parents** say their child faced inappropriate pressure/exploitation (highest of the three).
- Rural families spend significantly **less** on primary and other sports.
- More than half of urban parents are amenable to moving the season for weather.

### 4.2 By race/ethnicity — p.45–59
- Highest volunteer rate among parents of minority children; they also volunteer more hours.
- ★ **More than 1 in 4 parents of minority children** report inappropriate pressure/exploitation.
- Parents of Black children have the highest belief their child can reach D1/pro.
- Parents of White & Hispanic children spend significantly **more** on the primary sport.

### 4.3 By child sex (Male / Female) — p.60–71
- Female children participate more months/yr; males do more free play, coach-led practice, games.
- Parents are more likely to **coach** boys than girls.
- Parents of **female athletes spend more** on the primary sport; parents of males spend more on secondary sports.
- ~2 in 10 report inappropriate pressure/exploitation regardless of child sex.

### 4.4 By household income — p.72–86
- ★ **PRIMARY-sport spend:** <$50k **$604** · $50–99k **$961** · $100k+ **$1,591**. (<$50k spends $357 less than the middle tier and **$987 less** than the top tier.)
- **OTHER-sport spend:** <$50k $286 · $50–99k $426 · $100k+ $771.
- Affluent kids play more months/yr; $100k+ parents feel the most pressure and are most satisfied.
- ★ **Climate cancellations hit high- AND low-income families disproportionately** ($100k+ families lost the most days — 11.0 total; lowest-income also elevated).
- Higher-income parents most amenable to moving the season (53.2% vs 44.0%).

### 4.5 By child age (6–10 / 11–14 / 15–18) — p.87–100
- Older kids play more months and more days/week.
- ★ **PRIMARY-sport spend rises sharply with age:** 6–10 **$697** · 11–14 **$1,131** · 15–18 **$1,346**. (Travel/lodging for teens 15–18 = $456 alone.)
- Parents of older athletes spend more time per sport-day.

### 4.6 By primary sport (Basketball / Soccer / Baseball / Tackle / Flag / Dance) — p.101–120
- ★ **Inappropriate pressure/exploitation by sport:** Flag football **26.3%**, Baseball 24.5%, Dance 23.9%, Basketball 23.8%, Tackle football 17.4%, **Soccer 11.5%** (low).
- ★ **PRIMARY-sport spend by sport:** **Dance $2,048** (2–4× others), Baseball $1,113, Soccer $910, Basketball $876, Tackle football $634, Flag football $444.
- Background-check confirmation highest for flag-football parents (80.7%).

### 4.7 By state (top-represented states) — p.121–136
- California parents feel the most pressure (from child and other parents).
- Florida kids play the most months/yr; TX & NY parents eat out the most.
- ~1 in 5 report inappropriate pressure/exploitation across states.
- >50% in CA/TX/FL/NY amenable to moving the season.

### 4.8 Regions of interest (Colorado / NYC 5 Boroughs vs U.S.) — p.137+
- ★ **1 in 4 parents in the NYC 5 boroughs** report inappropriate pressure/exploitation (highest sub-sample).
- 5-borough parents engage the most volunteer hours, eat out the most, and are the most satisfied with sport culture; Colorado parents spend more time per sport-day.
- 5-borough families spend significantly more on "other" sports.

## NOTES & CAVEATS FOR PUBLICATION
- **Self-report:** all figures are parent estimates (spending, minutes, days) and carry recall/estimation error; treat means alongside the wide SDs/ranges noted above.
- **Oversampling:** Colorado and NYC five boroughs were intentionally oversampled; national percentages in Chapters 2–3 are weighted to be nationally representative, but the raw state counts are not.
- **Superscript letters** (a/b/c) in Chapter 4 tables denote statistically significant group differences; groups sharing a letter are not significantly different.
- The "$1,015.64 primary-sport average" here is the **same survey** behind the widely-cited "$1,016 / +46% since 2019" Project Play headline; the report itself does not restate the 2019 comparison (that came from Project Play's Feb 2025 writeup).
- Five participants identified as non-binary (parents) and two children as non-binary — too few for subgroup analysis.
